What did we learn today?


The author's purpose is the reason why they write a story or passage. They might want to tell you something new, make you laugh, or help you think about something.
The author writes a story to:

  • Inform: Teach or give facts (e.g., explaining how coal engines worked).
  • Entertain: Make the story fun and enjoyable (e.g., describing the exciting chugging sounds of trains).
  • Persuade: Convince us to think or do something (not present in the coal engine story).

How Do We Find the Purpose?

  • Look at the facts or details (e.g., "These engines worked by burning coal").
  • Check for descriptive words (e.g., "puffs of steam into the sky").
  • See if the text explains why something is important (e.g., "Coal engines helped people travel and carry goods").
